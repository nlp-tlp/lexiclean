This section of the basic tutorial will guide you through enhancing your dataset's quality by identifying and correcting low-quality text segments, such as out-of-vocabulary (OOV) words. LexiClean simplifies this task by automatically highlighting OOV words in orange, while in-vocabulary (IV) words are displayed in a standard color. You can edit and interact with tokens by right-clicking on them.

## Editing Your First Token

When you encounter an orange token, such as `c/o`, it indicates an out-of-vocabulary word, signifying non-standard or noisy text. To edit this token, left-click on it and change its content to `change out`. Upon editing, a popover will appear, showing the transformation you're about to make. After making your edit, left-click the `Apply` button to save the change. This process is known as a **Replacement**, a type of **Token Transformation** in LexiClean. Following the replacement, the token will turn green, indicating the successful update. Additionally, a bar will appear beneath the token; clicking this bar reveals token-level operations available for further actions, though we'll explore these in more detail later (for the eager, find out more [here](../interface/annotation/tokens)).
